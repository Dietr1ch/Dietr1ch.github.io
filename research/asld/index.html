What is this talk about?

Linked Data

What is it?

  • A way to publish structured data
  • Built over HTTP, IRI, and RDF

Why?

  • Humans are capable to gather information from the web by navigating it
    • They get to some starting site
    • By inspecting the site, they discover other sites
    • and traverse those sites until they find what they wanted
  • This differs heavily from what machines can do today
    • Machines do not trully understand language or the world (yet)
    • But they are astonishingly fast and accurate on properly structured data

Why do machines fail?

  • The real problem is that machines cannot traverse data.
    • The do know about the links, but they lack meaning for them.

How to solve it?

  • Today the Web is a graph of Websites connected by hyperlinks.
    • But the meaning of the links is encrypted on the text and it's context.
  • RDF sets up a framework for modeling the Web in a machine-friendly way.

RDF

It stands for Resource Description Framework

  • It describes a graph with machine identifyable nodes and links.
